03   ENVIRONMENT ART DIRECTION
Revision Process

01 — Initial Version

For Tao's apartment, the environment needed to feel old, lived-in, and slightly oppressive in support of the story. In the initial version, the central door drew attention away from the door on the right, where the interaction takes place, while the high saturation made the hallway feel too new.

02 — Revision

I reduced the central door's saturation, removed elements that did not fit the residential setting, and adjusted the perspective so the interaction area read more clearly. Muted lighting and color helped establish a more believable sense of age and unease.

03 — Final Adjustment

The final version expands the visible space and adds advertisements, worn surfaces, and door-frame patterns. These details give Tao's apartment more history, strengthen the residential atmosphere, and support the scene's narrative context.
